## Grammar Review

### 1) Turn a rough note into a full sentence

- Rough note: `revising Keyword Grounding Rules some copy phrasing was strategy-led instead of keyword-led`
- Better: `I'm revising the Keyword Grounding Rules because some copy phrasing was strategy-led instead of keyword-led.`
- Note: add `the`, a subject, and a connector like `because` to make the sentence complete.

### 2) Use `'s` for possession

- Correct: `This is your father's watch.`
- Note: apostrophe + `s` shows ownership.

### 3) `sunflower` is one word

- Wrong split: `sun flower`
- Correct: `sunflower`
- Note: many common compound nouns are written as one word.

### 4) `no difference` needs a full sentence pattern

- Incorrect: `That's no difference.`
- Natural: `There's no difference.`
- Also natural: `It makes no difference.` / `It's the same.`
- Note: if you use `different`, say `It's no different.`

### 5) Add `the` in `This part of the job I love`

- Less natural: `This part of job I love.`
- Correct: `This part of the job I love.`
- More common: `I love this part of the job.`
- Note: `job` is a specific singular noun here, so `the` fits naturally.

### 6) Use simple past with a finished time clause

- Incorrect: `I have purchased a metro card when I first come here.`
- Correct: `I purchased a metro card when I first came here.`
- Also natural: `I purchased the metro card when I first came here.`
- Note: `when I first came here` is a specific past time, so do not use present perfect.

### 7) Special question word order needs `do`

- Incorrect: `How much I have to pay?`
- Correct: `How much do I have to pay?`
- Note: the pattern is question word + `do/does` + subject + base verb.

### 8) `put out` is not `脱掉`

- If you mean 脱掉衣服: `Take off your clothes.`
- If you mean 把衣服拿出来/摆出来: `Put out your clothes.` / better `Lay out your clothes.`
- Note: phrasal verbs change meaning a lot, so memorize them as chunks.

### 9) Write the contraction correctly

- Incorrect writing: `IM downstairs.`
- Correct: `I'm downstairs.`
- Note: `I'm` needs the apostrophe.

## Pronunciation Tips

- `father's` /ˈfɑːðərz/: make the `th` voiced like in `this`, not voiceless like in `think`.
- `sunflower` /ˈsʌnˌflaʊər/: say it as one word and keep the stress on `sun`.
- `bubbly` /ˈbʌb.li/: use a short `u` sound; do not confuse it with `puppy`.
- `metro` /ˈmetroʊ/: say `me-tro` clearly in `metro card`.
- `I'm` /aɪm/: the apostrophe is silent, but it must be written in the contraction.
