## Grammar Review

### 1) Use an article with `Uber` when you mean a ride

- Less natural: `I would like to take Uber.`
- Better: `I'd like to take an Uber.` / `I'd like to get an Uber.`
- If you mean the service: `I'd like to use Uber.`
- Note: when `Uber` means a ride, English usually needs `an`.

### 2) `Almost every cruise ship` is a phrase, not a full sentence

- Phrase only: `Almost every cruise ship.`
- Full sentence: `Almost every cruise ship has restaurants and shows.`
- Note: this expression means `几乎每一艘邮轮`, but by itself it is not a complete sentence.

### 3) `Look at` fits better than `Look to` here

- Unnatural: `Look to the grab.`
- Better: `Look at the grab.`
- Note: `grab` works only if you really mean a mechanical claw or grab bucket.

### 4) `ego` and `eagle` are different words

- If you mean 鹰: `That is an eagle.`
- If you mean 自我/自尊心: `ego`
- More natural examples: `That's his ego.` / `That's ego talking.`
- Note: `That is an ego.` is grammatical, but it is not the most natural everyday wording.

### 5) The fishing word is `cutthroat`, not `route`

- Misheard forms: `cast route` / `cut route`
- Better: `That's my first cutthroat in Alaska.`
- Clearer: `That's my first cutthroat trout in Alaska.`
- Note: `cutthroat` here refers to a kind of trout.

### 6) Read `12:46` as a time, not a four-digit number

- Correct reading: `twelve forty-six`
- Note: for clock time, read the hour and minutes directly.

### 7) `rain` and `run` change the meaning completely

- `It's going to rain.` = 要下雨了
- `It's going to run.` = 它要开始跑了 / 运转了
- Note: this is a listening and pronunciation difference, not just a spelling difference.

### 8) `repeated competitor evidence` sounds too compressed

- Unnatural: `repeated competitor evidence`
- Better if the same item appears more than once: `duplicate competitor evidence`
- Better if you mean proof about a competitor's repeated actions: `repeated evidence of competitor activity`
- Note: with `evidence`, English often uses `of` to show what the evidence is about. The original phrase is understandable, but not natural business English.

## Pronunciation Tips

- `Uber` /ˈuːbər/: stress the first syllable: `OO-ber`.
- `cruise` /kruːz/: keep the long `oo` sound; do not shorten it.
- `bucks` /bʌks/: use the short vowel in `cut`, not the `oo` sound.
- `bear spray` /ber spreɪ/: connect the `spr` sound smoothly in `spray`.
- `cutthroat` /ˈkʌtˌθroʊt/: make the `th` clear in `throat`.
- `trout` /traʊt/: it rhymes with `out`.
- `eagle` /ˈiːɡəl/ vs `ego` /ˈiːɡoʊ/: the first syllable is similar, but the endings are different.
