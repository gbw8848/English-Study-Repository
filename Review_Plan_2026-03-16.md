## Grammar Review

### 1) `should not + base verb` works well for suggestions and strategy decisions

- Correct: `We should not redesign every SEO page from scratch.`
- Natural contraction: `We shouldn't redesign every SEO page from scratch.`
- Softer business alternative: `We don't need to redesign every SEO page from scratch.`
- Note: use `should not` when you want to give advice; use `don't need to` when you want to stress that something is unnecessary.

### 2) Turn short SEO note fragments into clear instruction sentences

- Fragment: `proof block style`
- Clearer note: `Use a proof-block style layout.`
- Fragment: `internal link slots`
- Clearer note: `Add internal link slots where they support the content.`
- Good caution: `Do not treat them as proven keyword targets without extra validation.`
- Note: short noun phrases are okay in rough notes, but full sentences are much clearer in documents and discussions.

### 3) Use `be worried about`, not `be worry about`

- Less natural: `I am so worry about this big ship.`
- Better: `I'm very worried about this big ship.`
- Stronger: `I'm worried that this big ship could be dangerous for many people.`

### 4) Ask about danger more directly

- Repetitive: `It's a big ship maybe dangerous for many people.`
- Better question: `Is the big ship dangerous for many people?`
- Better statement: `The ship could be dangerous for many people in a storm.`
- Note: use `could` or `might` once; repeating `maybe` makes the sentence harder to follow.

### 5) `improve safety` is natural, but `guarantee safety` has limits

- Less natural: `The captain can improve the safe, but he can't grant it's okay.`
- Better: `The captain can improve safety, but he can't guarantee that everything will be okay.`
- Note: `safety` is the noun; `safe` is usually an adjective.

### 6) Use `be built to handle`

- Less natural: `The big ship is built not to handle such huge waves.`
- Better: `The ship is not built to handle such huge waves.`
- Also natural: `I don't think the ship is built to handle waves that big.`

### 7) Keep the ship-safety terms fixed

- Correct terms: `big waves`, `watertight door`, `life jacket`
- Example: `If huge waves hit the ship, put on a life jacket and move to a lower area.`

### 8) `corner` and `angle` are not the same thing

- `corner` = the place where two sides or walls meet
- `angle` = the shape or degree between two lines
- Examples: `Stay away from the corners.` / `This is a right angle.`

## Pronunciation Tips

- `reference` stresses the first syllable: `REF-er-ence`.
- `validation` has a clear `day` sound in the middle: `val-i-DAY-shun`.
- `captain` is often pronounced like `CAP-tin` in fast speech.
- `waves` rhymes with `caves`.
- `watertight` links smoothly as `water` + `tight`.
- `life jacket` needs a clear long `i` sound in `life`.
- `avoid` stresses the second syllable: `uh-VOID`.
